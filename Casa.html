Experiences

In 2nd year of Baccalaureate, I had the opportunity to carry out a project with a microbit board that turned out to be a transformative experience for both me and our school community. The idea arose when I noticed that students had poor performance for learning mathematics in a non-traditional way. I decided to take the initiative and gathered a team of colleagues passionate about programming and technology. Together, we programmed on the microbit board to create an educational game. Once we launched the game, we saw an immediate impact. Mathematics learning improved significantly, students felt more empowered in their learning process. In addition to the personal satisfaction of creating something useful for the community, this project gave me development skills in programming, project management

Development of a Mobile Application for the Educational Community

During my second year, I had the opportunity to participate in a mobile application development project that not only improved the lives of students in our educational community, but also provided me with valuable programming experience. The project arose from a common need among students: to stay up to date with events, class schedules, and important announcements from the university. Together with a group of colleagues passionate about programming, we decided to tackle this challenge. Here are the key steps we followed: Needs survey: We conducted surveys and interviews with our colleagues to understand their main concerns and requirements for a mobile application that would help them in their student life. Design and Planning: We used the feedback collected to design an application that included an academic calendar, an event and announcement notification system, and the ability to access class schedules online. App Development: We use mobile app development tools and programming languages ​like Java and html to create the app from scratch. We worked closely to ensure the app was intuitive and easy to use. Testing and feedback: We invite a group of colleagues to test the application in its beta phase and collect their feedback to make improvements and corrections. Launch and Promotion: Once the app was ready, we launched it on the iOS and Android app stores. We organized promotional events on campus to inform students about the new tool.
